أَفْلَجُ ذ
, (TA,) or أَفْلَجُ الأَسْنَانِ, (S, Mgh, O, K, TA,) applied to a man, and فَلْجَآءُ الأَسْنَانِ applied to a woman, (S, O,) for the teeth must be mentioned, (IDrd, S, O, K,) [but MF disputes this,] and الأَسْنَانِ, applied to a man, accord. to one reading of a trad., (TA,) Having the teeth separate, one from another: (TA:) or, distant, or wide apart, one from another: (Mgh, * K:) or having the medial and lateral incisors distant, one from another, or wide apart. (S, O.) [See also أَفْرَقُ.] And الثَّنَايَا A man having an interstice between the middle pair of teeth; (S, O, K;) as also الثَّنَايَا; (A;) contr. of مُتَرَاصُّ الثَّنَايَا. (S, O.) And A woman that makes open spaces between her front teeth, for the purpose of improving their appearance. (L, from a trad., in which a curse is pronounced against her who does this.) And ثَغْرٌ أَفْلَجُ Front teeth that are separate, or distant, or wide apart, one from another; and signifies the same [app. when they are rendered so artificially: see فَلَجٌ]. (TA.) -b2- And أَفْلَجُ applied to a man, Having a crookedness, or curvature, [or bowing outwards,] in the arms: when it is in the legs, the person is termed أَفْحَجُ: (L:) or wide between the arms: (O, K:) or wide between the paps; (S, L;) which last explanation is said in the K to be erroneous; but he who is wide between the paps is also wide between the arms. (MF.) -b3- هِنٌ أَفْلَجُ A vulva, of a woman, whereof the labia majora are wide apart. (L.) -b4- فَرَسٌ أَفْلَجُ A horse having the prominent parts of the haunch-bones wide apart. (IDrd, O, L.)
[
أَفْلَجِىٌّ ذ
Having the fingers wide apart. (Freytag, from the Deewán of the Hudhalees.)]
مُفْلَجٌ ذ
[Rendered] successful, or victorious; and safe, or secure. (KL.) [See also its verb.]
مُفَلَّجٌ ذ
: see أَفْلَجُ, in three places. -b2- أَمْرٌ مُفَلَّجٌ An affair not rightly disposed or directed. (O, K.)
مَفْلُوجٌ ذ
Having the disease termed الفَالِجٌ. (S, Mgh, O, Msb, K.)
مُتَفَلِّجَةٌ ذ
